# Trommel Panel Wear Inspection — January 2026

## Inspection summary

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Inspection date | 2026-01-14 |
| Panels in service since | 2025-03-10 |
| Service life so far | ~10 months |
| Drums inspected | Both mill-feed trommels |
| Sampling | 20 panels per drum, distributed across drum length |

## Wear measurements

| Zone (along drum) | Original thickness | Measured thickness | Wear |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feed end (0–1.2 m) | 40 mm | 31 mm | 22.5% |
| Mid section (1.2–2.4 m) | 40 mm | 35 mm | 12.5% |
| Discharge end (2.4–3.6 m) | 40 mm | 37 mm | 7.5% |

## Projected wear life

At feed-end wear rate (≈0.9 mm/month), feed-end panels are projected to
reach the 15 mm minimum service thickness (per
`rules/design_rules.md` §"Minimum service thickness") at approximately
**16–17 months** total service life. Mid and discharge sections project
significantly longer (30+ months).

## Observed issue

Feed-end panels show **accelerated, uneven wear** concentrated on the
leading edge of each panel, consistent with direct high-velocity ore
impingement at the feed point rather than general abrasion. No panel
cracking, pin failure, or blinding observed. Mid and discharge sections
show normal, even abrasive wear consistent with expected PU performance
on this ore type.

## Open question

Whether the feed-end wear pattern warrants a localized harder-compound
or thicker panel zone at the feed end only, or a feed-chute
modification to reduce impingement velocity, is not yet determined —
see `templates/analysis_brief_template.md` for how this should be
analyzed before any panel spec change is made.
