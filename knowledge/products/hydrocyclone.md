# Tega Hydrocyclone Liner

## Description

Wear-resistant liner set for hydrocyclone classification units
(cone and cylinder sections), used to protect the cyclone body from
abrasive slurry wear during particle classification.

## Available specifications

| Parameter | Range / Options |
|---|---|
| Cyclone diameter range | 150 mm – 1000 mm |
| Liner material | Polyurethane (cast), natural rubber |
| Liner thickness | 12 mm – 30 mm depending on cyclone size |
| Sections | Inlet head, cylinder, cone, apex/spigot |
| Apex/spigot liner | Ceramic-PU composite (high-wear zone) |
| Fixing | Bonded + mechanical retention ring |

## Material guidance

| Slurry condition | Recommended liner material |
|---|---|
| Fine, low-solids slurry | Rubber (cost-effective, adequate life) |
| Coarse, high-solids, abrasive slurry | Polyurethane or ceramic-PU composite (apex/spigot) |
| High-temperature slurry | Rubber (PU degrades faster above ~70°C) |

## Wear-prone zone

The apex/spigot section experiences the highest velocity and wear rate
in any hydrocyclone and should be inspected on a shorter interval than
the cylinder/cone sections — see `rules/standards.md`
§"Inspection intervals".

## Known field installations

Greenfield Aggregates Ltd (`customers/customer_002/profile.md`) has one
4-unit cluster fitted with rubber liners, installed 2024 — no wear
report on file yet.
